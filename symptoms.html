5. Symptoms and Warning Signs

1. Symptoms During Seizures

Seizure symptoms vary depending on seizure type and brain region involved.

Focal (Partial) Seizures

  • Focal Aware Seizures: Tingling, unusual sensations, emotional changes, or muscle jerking.
  • Focal Impaired Awareness Seizures: Confusion, blank staring, repetitive movements, memory loss.

Generalized Seizures

  • Absence Seizures: Brief staring spells and unresponsiveness.
  • Tonic Seizures: Sudden muscle stiffening.
  • Clonic Seizures: Repetitive jerking movements.
  • Tonic-Clonic Seizures: Loss of consciousness, body stiffening, jerking movements.
  • Myoclonic Seizures: Sudden muscle jerks.
  • Atonic Seizures: Sudden loss of muscle tone leading to falls.

2. Warning Signs (Aura or Prodrome)

  • Unusual smells, tastes, or visual disturbances
  • Dizziness or nausea
  • Sudden fear or anxiety
  • Confusion or restlessness

3. Post-Seizure Symptoms (Postictal Phase)

  • Confusion or disorientation
  • Extreme tiredness
  • Headache or body soreness
  • Temporary memory loss
