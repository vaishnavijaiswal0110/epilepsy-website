Introduction

Epilepsy is a chronic neurological disorder characterized by recurrent seizures caused by abnormal electrical activity in the brain. These seizures may vary in severity and can influence movement, behavior, awareness, or sensation.

Under normal conditions, neurons communicate through controlled electrical and chemical signals. In epilepsy, this communication becomes irregular, leading to sudden and uncontrolled brain activity.

Key Aspects of Epilepsy

  • Causes: Genetic conditions, head injuries, brain infections, or unknown factors.
  • Types of Seizures: Focal seizures and generalized seizures.
  • Symptoms: Loss of awareness, confusion, muscle jerking, and staring spells.
  • Diagnosis: EEG tests, MRI or CT scans, and clinical evaluation.
  • Treatment: Anti-epileptic drugs, lifestyle management, and surgery in severe cases.
