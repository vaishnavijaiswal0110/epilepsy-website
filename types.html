4. Types of Seizures

Seizures are classified based on where abnormal electrical activity begins in the brain and how it spreads.

Focal Seizures

Focal seizures begin in one specific area of the brain.

  • Focal Aware Seizures: The person remains conscious and may experience unusual sensations, emotional changes, or muscle jerking.
  • Focal Impaired Awareness Seizures: Consciousness is affected. The person may appear confused, stare blankly, or perform repetitive movements.
  • Focal to Bilateral Tonic-Clonic Seizures: Focal seizures that spread to both sides of the brain and cause loss of consciousness and full body convulsions.

Generalized Seizures

Generalized seizures involve both sides of the brain from the beginning.

  • Absence Seizures: Brief staring spells with loss of awareness.
  • Tonic Seizures: Sudden muscle stiffening.
  • Clonic Seizures: Repetitive jerking movements.
  • Tonic-Clonic Seizures: Loss of consciousness followed by stiffening and jerking movements.
  • Myoclonic Seizures: Sudden brief muscle jerks.
  • Atonic Seizures: Sudden loss of muscle tone causing falls.

Unknown-Onset Seizures

In some cases, the beginning of a seizure cannot be clearly identified. These seizures are classified as unknown-onset seizures until further medical evaluation.
